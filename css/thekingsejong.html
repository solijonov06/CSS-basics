THE KING SEJONG STORY HALL

King Sejong Story Hall is an exhibition space located beneath Gwanghwamun Square in Seoul, South Korea.
It is dedicated to the life and achievements of King Sejong the Great (세종대왕, 1397–1450), the fourth ruler of the Joseon Dynasty and one of Korea's most revered historical figures.



What to Expect at King Sejong Story Hall 🔹 Hangul Invention: Learn about King Sejong’s most famous achievement—creating the Korean alphabet, Hangul (한글), in 1443 to promote literacy among commoners. Exhibits explain the principles behind its design.
🔹 Scientific and Technological Innovations: Displays showcase Sejong’s contributions to astronomy, meteorology, printing, and military technology.
🔹 Interactive Exhibits: The hall features multimedia presentations, models, and digital experiences to engage visitors in learning about Sejong’s impact.
🔹 Yi Sun-sin Hall: This space is also connected to an exhibition about Admiral Yi Sun-sin (이순신), a legendary naval commander known for his victories against Japan during the late 16th century.


Location & Accessibility 📍 Where: Beneath Gwanghwamun Square, near Gyeongbokgung Palace 🚇 Subway: Gwanghwamun Station (Line 5), Exit 2
